Opening Range Breakout (ORB) Strategy
======================================

Instruments:      MES (Micro E-mini S&P 500), M2K (Micro E-mini Russell 2000)
Direction:        Long only
Backtest period:  2019-2026 (7 years, ~1,500 RTH sessions per instrument)


CONCEPT
-------
The Opening Range Breakout captures directional momentum after the market resolves
its initial price discovery. The first 15 minutes of the RTH session (9:30-9:45 ET)
establish the "opening range" -- a high/low band that the market frequently uses as
a reference for the rest of the morning. When price breaks cleanly above that range
in the follow-through window, it tends to continue.

Only long (upside) breakouts are traded. Short breakouts have been consistently
negative in backtesting and are not implemented.


ENTRY RULES
-----------

1. Opening Range

   Record the high and low across the first three 5-minute bars (9:30, 9:35, 9:40 ET,
   i.e. bars closing at 9:35, 9:40, 9:45 ET).

     ORB high  = highest high of those three bars
     ORB low   = lowest low of those three bars
     ORB width = ORB high - ORB low

2. Width Filter

   Only trade if the ORB is "wide" -- in the top third of historical ORB widths
   for that instrument.

     MES:  >= 15.25 pts
     M2K:  >= 14.30 pts

   Narrow opening ranges produce low-quality breakouts. The wide-tertile filter
   eliminates roughly 2/3 of sessions and concentrates trades in the clearest setups.

3. Fire Window

   A breakout is only valid if it occurs in the morning fire window:
   9:45 - 10:30 ET  (one trade per session).

4. Breakout Trigger

   When the 5-minute bar closes above the ORB high, enter long at the close of
   that bar.


EXIT RULES
----------
Exits use a 2-sigma stop and 2-sigma target, where sigma (s) is the rolling
close-return standard deviation computed from the 20 most recent 5-minute bars
(100 minutes), expressed in points.

     Entry:   Close of trigger bar
     Target:  Entry + 2s
     Stop:    Entry - 2s
     Time:    Force-close after 25 minutes if neither level is hit

Stop and target are resting bracket orders (exchange-managed OCO); the time exit
is a fallback only.


BACKTEST RESULTS
----------------
Wide ORB, morning breakout (9:45-10:30 ET), 2s stop / 2s target:

     Instrument   Trades (7yr)   Win rate   EV per trade   Total R
     ----------   ------------   --------   ------------   -------
     MES          ~280           ~53%       +0.35-0.44R    ~+100R
     M2K          ~280           ~56%       +0.61R         +170R

M2K outperforms MES in this setup -- Russell 2000 tends to trend more cleanly
after opening range resolution.

R = risk unit = 1x (2s stop distance).

Tight stops placed at the ORB high (breakout extension only) were consistently
negative (EV = -0.08 to -0.38R) and are not used. The sigma-based stop is required.


PARAMETERS AT A GLANCE
-----------------------
  Range period    15 min (9:30-9:45 ET)
  Bar timeframe   5-minute, clock-aligned
  Volatility (s)  Rolling 20-bar close-return std dev
  Stop            2.0s below entry
  Target          2.0s above entry
  Max hold        25 minutes
  Direction       Long only
  Trades per day  0 or 1 per instrument


NOTES
-----
- The morning breakout window (9:45-10:30 ET) is the only active window. A "power
  hour" afternoon window (14:30-15:00 range, 15:00-16:00 fire) was also tested but
  showed negative expected value for both instruments and was not implemented.

- Because sigma is computed live at the time of the breakout, the stop/target
  distances adapt to the current session's volatility rather than using fixed points.

- A position guard prevents the ORB trade and a separate momentum continuation
  signal from being open simultaneously on the same instrument.
